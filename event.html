Events

Thoughtfullness, empathy and careful consideration saw the initiation of the Celebral palsy support programme(CPSP) headed by Mrs.Everlyne despite the covid 19 pandemic, it did not stop her from extending support to the family of Jackline. She is a mother of two boys:Alfonse and Antony of ages seven and six respectively. Their mother Jackline double plays as their care giver and breadwinner. Unfortunately ,the current global pandemic situation has halted her sources of income.We come together to help women like Jackline through the hard times.

Preparation for the visit to her house and other women like her begins two weeks prior to the actual visit date on the 6th June 2020.Mobilization of financial support from a few friends of Cerebral Palsy Support Program helped Mrs.Everlyne raise adequate funds to
facilitate the donation of substantial food supply to women like
Jackline and her two sons.This helps such mothers provide a regular healthy diet that is of necessity to any Celebral palsy child.

Jackline and other women like her express their gratitude for the given support. She also shares her story how the experience was raising two children with the condition.Mothers find it hard to maintain regular visits to the doctor, leave alone providing a good healthy diet.
This explains the stagnated state of the two boys.We visit ladies like Jackline, encouraging them never to give up.

Cerebral Palsy Support Program has always been glad to help out the society especially those living with CP.
We fight the stigma that families with members living with the condition go through and the caregivers.
Let us join hands to make the world a better place for everyone.
Better means better for everyone.

Volunteer Work

Donations is not all about money, but time also. Being part of the program will be a major boost not only to us but also to the affected just by spending time with them.

How can YOU make a difference?

  • Organize for events that are coming up, by helping spread the information.
  • Be part of the events, going to visit the caregivers and the children at a their homes to encourage them and spend time with them.
  • Creating awareness and spreading information about CP.
  • Organizing for donations from the community at large.
