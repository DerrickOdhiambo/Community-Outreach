CELEBRAL PALSY SYMPTOMS

What do we know about this celebral palsy , this is a group of disorders that affect a person's ability to move
and maintain balance and posture.The symptoms of celebral palsy commonly referred to as cp vary from person to person
The exact symptoms might change over a person's lifetime,the signs of cp vary greatly because there are many different types and levels of disability.Let's get to understand this better:

  • The main sign that a child might have cp is a delay reaching motor or movement milestones(such as rolling over,sitting,standing or walking).
  • In a baby younger than six months of age
    ,the baby's head lags when you pick him up while he's lying on his back
  • The baby feels floppy(confused)
  • The baby feels stiff
  • When the baby is held in your arms he seems to be overextended his back and neck, constantly acting as if he is pushing away from you.
  • When you pick the baby up the baby's legs get stiffy and they cross or scissor(the leg movements suggest opening and closing of scissors)

In a baby older than 6 months of
Age

  • The baby doesn't roll over in either direction
  • The baby cannot bring her hands together
  • The baby has difficulty bringing her hands to the mouth
  • The baby reaches out with only one hand while keeping the other fisted

In a baby older than 10 months of
age

  • The baby crawls in a lopsided manner,pushing off with one hand and leg while dragging the opposite hand and leg
  • The baby scoots around on his buttocks or hops on his knees ,but does not crawl on all fours

We strongly advice you consult your child's doctor or nurse if you notice any of these signs
